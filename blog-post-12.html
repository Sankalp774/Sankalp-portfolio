Timeline · December 2025

I Finally Put My Work Somewhere the World Could See

On December 12, 2025, I pushed My-Portfolio to GitHub Pages — and it felt like moving from notebooks to a storefront window. Until then, my projects were impressive in isolation but invisible as a story. The first portfolio version was not the cinematic site you see today; it was an honest attempt to connect repos, resume, and identity in one place. Deploying on GitHub Pages taught me the mechanics I had postponed: build pipelines, asset paths, cache busting, and the anxiety of refreshing a live URL at 1 a.m. I kept iterating into February 2026 because a portfolio is never truly done; it is a living mirror of your timeline. That first deployment also forced clarity — which projects matter, which skills are current, which narrative recruiters should grasp in thirty seconds. The Leclerc-inspired redesign came later, but this December push was the commitment: I am not only building repositories, I am curating a public arc. Every creative feature on this site — certificates grid, exploration HUD, cinematic heroes — traces back to that decision to show up online with intention. A portfolio is not decoration; it is the frame around everything else you build. Sharing the URL with friends was scarier than any viva. Their feedback hurt in useful ways and pushed me toward the cinematic rebuild you are browsing now. Public work invites public improvement. From the first plain layout to this cinematic version, the portfolio tracks my taste maturing in public view.
